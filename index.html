Preserving our roots in a changing world



What is culture?

Culture can be defined as all the ways of life including arts, beliefs and institutions of a population that are passed down from generation to generation. Culture has been called "the way of life for an entire society." As such, it includes codes of manners, dress, language, religion, rituals, art.

How do we preserve the cultures that are gone?

We can interview elders in your community to create a cultural record of your beliefs and traditions that you can pass on to others. We can also preserve traditional customs by participating in practices like cooking recipes passed down through the generations or teaching children how to dance traditional dances.

What happens when a culture is gone/lost?

The loss of a culture weakens a community's ties to its history, interferes with the passing on of customs and knowledge, and erases the material and immaterial symbols of its identity. Identity, traditional knowledge, and distinctive languages are all gone when a culture is wiped out. Younger generations may become disconnected from their heritage, and communities may feel alienated. This loss weakens our knowledge of the past, removes important environmental practices, and decreases world variety.
